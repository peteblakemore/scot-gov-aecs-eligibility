Apply for the Agri-Environment Climate Scheme (AECS)

Follow these steps to apply for funding to help you manage your land in a way that supports biodiversity, improves water quality and reduces carbon emissions.

Read the scheme guidance to check you're eligible.

  1. 1 Register for rural payments

    You need a Business Reference Number (BRN) to apply for funding.

    1. Register with Rural Payments and Inspections Division (RPID)

      Register with RPID Online

    2. Get your BRN
  2. 2 Check what you can apply for

    Find out which options are available for your land:

    • Use the eligibility checker to see what's available for your holding
    • Read about the management options and capital items you can apply for

    You may need permission from NatureScot if your land is outside a target area. Check if you need an endorsement.

  3. 3 Get your documents ready

    You must complete a Farm Environment Assessment (FEA) before you apply. Read the FEA guidance.

    You'll also need:

    • habitat assessments
    • photographs of your land
    • any required permissions or consents

    Contact NatureScot before you apply if you:

    • have land in a designated site
    • manage deer
    • need permission to apply for an option
  4. 4 Apply online

    Submit your application through Rural Payments and Services.

    You'll need to:

    • sign in to your account
    • fill in the application form
    • upload your supporting documents
    • check your answers
    • submit your application

    Start your application

  5. 5 What happens next

    If your application is successful, we'll send you:

    • a contract to sign and return
    • a management diary
    • permission to start work

    You can ask for a review if your application is not successful.

  6. 6 Manage your agreement

    Once your agreement starts you must:

    • claim your payments each year
    • record all activities in your management diary
    • tell us about any changes to your agreement
